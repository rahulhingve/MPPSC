📘 Fundamental Rights & Directive Principles

Teacher ne pehle Articles 15, 16, 17, 23, 24, 25, 29, 46 aur 350A ko ST welfare ke angle se revise karaya.

Article 15: Equality / Bhedbhav Nahi

  • Religion, race/origin, caste, sex/gender, place of birth ke basis par discrimination nahi.
  • 15(2): public wells, tanks, shops, hotels, roads jaise public places par kisi ko roka nahi ja sakta.
  • 15(3): women aur children ke liye special provisions ban sakte hain; Article 15 ka violation nahi maana jayega.
  • 15(4): ST/educationally backward groups ki educational upliftment ke liye special provision.
  • 15(5): admission/enrolment mein special provisions, government aur private educational institutions dono mein.

Article 16: Public Employment

  • 16: lok niyojan / government employment mein equality of opportunity.
  • 16(4): backward classes ke appointments/posts mein reservation ka provision.
  • 16(4A): promotion mein reservation.
  • 16(4B): backlog posts carry forward honge aur 50% limit mein count nahi honge.
  • Backlog ka simple meaning: bache hue reserved posts.

Article 46 & Cultural Rights

  • Article 46: State SC/ST aur weak sections ke educational-economic interests ko promote karega.
  • Social injustice aur exploitation se protection dena state ka duty hai.
  • Article 29: language, script aur culture ko protect karne ka right.
  • Article 350A: primary level par mother tongue education ki facility, especially linguistic minorities ke liye.

Labour, Freedom & Religion

  • Article 17: untouchability / chhuachhut ka end; SC/ST Act 1989 isi direction mein linked hai.
  • Article 18: titles / upadhiyon ka end.
  • Article 19: speech, movement, residence, association aur profession/business freedoms.
  • Article 23: bonded labour, forced labour, begar allowed nahi.
  • Article 24: 14 saal se kam bachchon se harmful work nahi karwaya jayega.
  • Article 25: religious freedom; teacher ne tribal temple-entry context se link kiya.
🔥 Exam Hot: 15(4) First Amendment 1951 se, 15(5) 93rd Amendment 2005 se, 16(4A) 77th Amendment 1995 se, aur 16(4B) 81st Amendment 2000 se linked hai.

🗺️ Article 244, Fifth Schedule & Sixth Schedule

Article 244: Scheduled Areas aur Tribal Areas ke administration ka core article. 244(1) Fifth Schedule, 244(2) Sixth Schedule se link hai.

244(1): Fifth Schedule

  • Scheduled Areas ke liye special provisions.
  • Scheduled Area declare karne ka power President ke paas.
  • India mein teacher ke according 10 states mein Scheduled Areas.
  • Tribal Advisory Council (TAC) banayi jaati hai.
  • TAC mein total 20 members; 3/4 members ST MLAs hone chahiye.

244(2): Sixth Schedule

  • Assam, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Tripura ke tribal areas ke special provisions.
  • In states mein Autonomous District Councils ka arrangement.
  • Teacher ne isko “swait jila parishad” ke naam se revise karaya.

🏛️ Article 338A, 339, 342, 366 & 164(1)

Article 338A: NCST

  • Article 338A mein National Commission for Scheduled Tribes.
  • 338 mein National Commission for Scheduled Castes.
  • 1978: executive order se SC/ST Commission.
  • 65th Amendment 1990: constitutional status.
  • 89th Amendment 2003: SC aur ST commissions separate.
  • NCST 2004 mein bana; first chairperson Kunwar Singh.
  • Composition: 1 Chairperson, 1 Vice-Chairperson, 3 Members.

MP ST Commission

  • Madhya Pradesh Anusuchit Janjati Aayog 1995 mein establish hua.
  • Teacher ne isko statutory/sanvidhik body bataya.
  • Statutory body normal law process se banti hai, constitutional body nahi.

Article 339: Welfare Commission

  • President Scheduled Areas ke administration aur ST welfare report ke liye commission appoint kar sakta hai.
  • Constitution commencement ke 10 saal ke andar bhi commission ka provision.
  • 1960 mein Dhebar Commission bana.
  • Dhebar Commission recommendations se very backward tribes / PVTG concept linked.
  • MP ke PVTG: Baiga, Bharia, Saharia.

Definition & Notification

  • Article 366(25): Scheduled Tribes / Janjati definition context.
  • Article 342: President kisi community ko Scheduled Tribe notify kar sakta hai.
  • MP mein pehle 46 ST groups; 2003 mein Parliament ne Keer, Meena, Pardhi ko remove kiya; ab 43.
  • Article 341: Scheduled Castes se linked.
  • Article 340: Backward classes commission; Kaka Kalelkar aur Mandal mention.

Article 164(1): Tribal Welfare Minister

  • Part VI: Articles 152 to 237 states se related.
  • State executive, legislature, judiciary isi range mein aate hain.
  • Separate tribal welfare ministry: MP, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, Jharkhand.
🔥 Exam Hot: 89th Amendment 2003 se NCST separate hua, lekin commission 2004 mein constituted hua.

🔥 Master Exam Hot Facts

Amendments

  • 1st Amendment 1951: Article 15(4).
  • 65th Amendment 1990: SC/ST Commission ko constitutional status.
  • 73rd Amendment 1992: Panchayats, Part IX, 243-243O.
  • 77th Amendment 1995: Article 16(4A), promotion reservation.
  • 81st Amendment 2000: Article 16(4B), backlog posts.
  • 89th Amendment 2003: NCST separate, 338A.
  • 93rd Amendment 2005: Article 15(5).
  • 104th Amendment 2019: SC/ST reservation extension; Anglo-Indian seats removed.

Numbers

  • Lok Sabha current elected seats: 543 after Anglo-Indian nominations removed.
  • Lok Sabha ST reserved total: 47.
  • MP Lok Sabha total: 29; MP ST Lok Sabha seats: 6.
  • MP Vidhan Sabha total: 230; ST 47; SC 35.
  • Scheduled Area states: 10.
  • TAC members: 20; 3/4 ST MLAs.
  • MP PVTG: 3; Baiga, Bharia, Saharia.
  • MP ST groups: earlier 46; after 2003 change 43.

Dates & Years

  • 26 January 1950: Constitution enforced.
  • 1960: Gujarat state formed; Dhebar Commission.
  • 1975: Sikkim state.
  • 1978: SC/ST Commission by executive order.
  • 1980: Bachpan Bachao Andolan.
  • 1986: Goa churches UNESCO World Heritage list mention.
  • 1987: Mizoram, Arunachal Pradesh, Goa states.
  • 1994: Dilip Singh Bhuria Committee.
  • 1995: MP ST Commission.
  • 1996: PESA Act.
  • 2004: NCST constituted.
  • 15 November 2022: MP PESA implementation start; Birsa Munda Jayanti.
