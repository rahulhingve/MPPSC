⚖️
3 Types of Provisions
Constitutional vs Statutory vs Executive
Type 1
संवैधानिक प्रावधान
Constitutional Provisions
Provisions that were part of the Constitution at the time of its making OR added later via Constitutional Amendment (Art. 368).

Examples: Art. 46, Art. 338A, Art. 15(4)
Type 2
सांविधिक प्रावधान
Statutory Provisions
Laws made by Parliament through ordinary legislation (simple/special majority) — NOT constitutional amendments.

Example: SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989
Type 3
कार्यकारी प्रावधान
Executive Provisions
Provisions made by the Executive (Government Orders/Schemes), not requiring legislation.

Example: National ST Commission (1978) — before it became constitutional via 89th Amendment
💡
Key Difference

Constitutional amendment needs Special Majority (2/3 members + 50% state ratification for some) under Art. 368 (Part 20). Ordinary laws need Simple/Absolute majority only. Art. 368 was borrowed from South Africa.

📖
Definition-Related Articles
Where is "Scheduled Tribe" defined?
Art. 366(25)
Part 19
Definition of Scheduled Tribes — Article 366 contains definitions of various terms used in the Constitution. Clause 25 gives the definition of STs. Note: The definition here is not fully clear/precise — it says STs are those notified under Article 342.
Art. 342(1)
Part 16
Power to Specify STs — The President, after consulting the Governor of a State (or Administrator of UT), may by public notification specify which communities are STs. Parliament can later amend this list.

📅 On 26 Jan 1950, President notified 705 tribes across India (Art. 342(1)). MP had 46 tribes initially, now 43. Odisha leads with 62 tribes.
🔍
Why "Scheduled" Tribes?

Because on 26 January 1950, these tribes were scheduled/listed (notified) under Art. 342(1) — hence the term "Anusuchit Janjati" (Scheduled Tribe / ST).

🎓
Educational & Cultural Rights
Articles in Part 3 (Fundamental Rights)
Art. 15
Part 3
Prohibition of Discrimination — No discrimination on grounds of religion, caste, sex, place of birth, or residence.
Art. 15(4) — Added by 1st Constitutional Amendment, 1951 — State may make special provisions for socially/educationally backward classes including SC/ST (e.g., reservations in engineering, medical colleges).
Art. 16
Part 3
Equality of Opportunity in Public Employment
Art. 16(4) — Reservations in appointments for backward/ST communities.
Art. 16(4A) — Reservation in promotions for SCs/STs.
Art. 16(4B) — Backlog posts — unfilled vacancies carried forward to next year.
Art. 29
Part 3
Protection of Interests of Minorities — Any group with a distinct language, script, or culture has the right to conserve it. This protects tribal culture, language, and traditions.
Art. 46
Part 4 (DPSP)
Promotion of Educational & Economic Interests — It is the State's duty (Directive Principle) to promote educational and economic interests of SCs, STs, and other weaker sections, and protect them from social injustice.
✅
Remember: Fundamental Rights vs DPSP

Arts. 14–18 (Equality), 19–22 (Freedom), 23–24 (Against Exploitation), 25–28 (Religion), 29–30 (Culture & Education), 32 (Constitutional Remedies) are Fundamental Rights (Part 3). Art. 46 is a Directive Principle (Part 4) — not enforceable in court but binding on State policy.

🤝
Social Protection Articles
Against exploitation, untouchability & child labour
Art. 17
Part 3
Abolition of Untouchability (छुआछूत का अंत) — Untouchability is abolished and its practice is a punishable offence. Protects tribal communities from caste-based discrimination.
Art. 23
Part 3
Prohibition of Bonded Labour (बंधुआ मजदूरी) — Traffic in human beings and forced labour (begar) are prohibited. Critical for tribal communities who were historically exploited as bonded labourers.
Art. 24
Part 3
Prohibition of Child Labour — No child below 14 years shall be employed in any factory or mine or engaged in any hazardous work. Protects tribal children from exploitation.
🧠
Trick: Art. 23 vs 24

"Person first, then Son" — Art. 23 = Adults (bonded labour) | Art. 24 = Children (child labour). Or: 23 = बंधुआ, 24 = बच्चे

💰
Economic Provisions
Financial support & grants for tribal welfare
Art. 275
Part 12
Grants from Union to States — Parliament may provide grants-in-aid from the Consolidated Fund of India to states for tribal welfare and development of Scheduled Areas. Central government provides money to state governments specifically for ST upliftment.
Art. 46
Part 4 (DPSP)
Economic Interests (DPSP) — State shall promote economic interests of SCs, STs and protect them from social injustice and all forms of exploitation. (Also listed under educational — dual purpose article.)
🗳️
Political Rights & Reservations
Representation in Lok Sabha, State Assembly, Panchayats & ULBs
Art. 330
Part 16
Reservation in Lok Sabha — Seats reserved for SCs and STs in Lok Sabha proportionate to their population.
🔢 MP sends 47 ST members to Lok Sabha
Art. 332
Part 16
Reservation in State Assemblies (Vidhan Sabha) — STs get reserved seats in State Legislative Assemblies. MP has 47 ST reserved seats in Vidhan Sabha.
Art. 334
Part 16
Reservation Duration — Originally, ST reservation was for 10 years from 1950. Extended multiple times. The 104th Constitutional Amendment (2019) extended it to 2030.
⚠️ The same amendment abolished the Anglo-Indian reserved seats.
Art. 243D
Part 9 (Panchayats)
Reservation in Panchayats — Seats reserved for STs in Gram Panchayat, Janpad Panchayat and Zila Panchayat. Also includes women's reservation.
🧠 Trick: D से "Daas" (दास) → poor/weaker sections → ST reservation in Panchayats
Art. 243T
Part 9A (ULBs)
Reservation in Urban Local Bodies — Seats reserved for STs in Nagar Panchayat, Nagar Palika and Nagar Nigam.
🧠 Trick: T → "Tree" (पेड़) → आरक्षण की छांव in Urban Bodies
📌
Part 9 (Panchayats) — Art. 243 to 243O

Part 9A (Urban Bodies) — Art. 243P to 243ZG | Part 16 = Art. 330 to 342 (Special Provisions for SC/ST)

🏛️
Administrative Provisions
Tribal Commissions, Tribal Ministry, Scheduled Areas
Art. 338A
Part 16
National Commission for Scheduled Tribes (NCST) — A constitutional body for safeguarding rights and welfare of STs.
📅 Originally created as an executive body in 1978. Made constitutional by the 89th Constitutional Amendment Act. Now a statutory commission under Art. 338A.
Art. 339
Part 16
Control of Union over Welfare of STs — The President may appoint a Commission to report on the administration of Scheduled Areas and welfare of STs. Must be done within 10 years of Constitution's commencement (also periodically thereafter).
Example: Dhebar Commission was appointed under this provision.
Art. 164(1)
Part 6
Tribal Affairs Ministry — In the states of MP, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand and Odisha, there shall be a Minister specifically in charge of Tribal Welfare.
🧠 Trick: "MP ke Chhattisgarh mein Jhad (Jhar) gir gaye, Ud ke Odisha pahunche" → MP + CG + Jharkhand + Odisha
Art. 244(1)
Part 10
Scheduled Areas Administration — Provisions of 5th Schedule apply to the administration of Scheduled Areas and Scheduled Tribes in states other than Assam, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Tripura.
Tribal Advisory Councils (जनजातीय सलाहकार परिषद) are established in these areas.
Art. 244(2)
Part 10
Tribal Areas Administration (NE States) — Provisions of 6th Schedule apply to the administration of tribal areas in Assam, Meghalaya, Mizoram, and Tripura.
🧠 Trick: "AMMT" or "एटीएम" → Assam + Meghalaya + Mizoram + Tripura
⚡
5th Schedule vs 6th Schedule — Key Difference
  • 5th Schedule → Covers Scheduled Areas in ALL states EXCEPT Assam, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Tripura
  • 6th Schedule → Covers Tribal Areas SPECIFICALLY in Assam, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Tripura

Special Provisions — Article 371 Series
State-specific tribal and regional protections
ℹ️
Why Art. 371?

Article 370 gave special status to J&K (now revoked). Similarly, Art. 371 and its sub-clauses give special provisions to various states (especially for tribal areas) to promote their development and protect their interests.

There is a demand to include Ladakh under Art. 371 to give it special tribal protections similar to these states. (Active political issue as of 2024)

📋
5th & 6th Schedules
Administration of Tribal / Scheduled Areas
Schedule 5
Panchami Anusuchi
Covers: Scheduled Areas in ALL states EXCEPT AMMT

Provisions: Governor's special powers, Tribal Advisory Council (TAC), Govt. can restrict/modify laws, no alienation of tribal land

Article: 244(1)
Schedule 6
Shashthami Anusuchi
Covers: Tribal Areas of Assam, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Tripura (AMMT)

Provisions: Autonomous District Councils (ADCs) with legislative powers, separate courts for tribal areas

Article: 244(2)

Memory Tricks (Pramod Sir's Method)
Art. 15
"15 August flag ceremony"
On 15 Aug, everyone gets ladoo equally — no discrimination by caste/religion/sex (Art. 15 = no discrimination)
Parts
"भाग 15 = चुनाव"
15 Aug → parties carry flags → elections → Part 15 = Elections (Art. 324–329)
Art. 164(1)
"MP mein CG ke jhad gaye, ud ke Odisha"
4 states needing Tribal Minister: Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, Odisha
Art. 244(2) / 6th Schedule
"AMMT / ATM"
Assam + Meghalaya + Mizoram + Tripura = "ATM mein janjatiya ko paise do"
Art. 23 vs 24
"Pehle aadmi, phir bacha"
Art. 23 = Adults (bonded labour) | Art. 24 = Children (child labour) — बड़ा नंबर = छोटा बच्चा ❌
Art. 243D vs 243T
"D = Daas (दास) | T = Tree (पेड़)"
D → poor/weaker (Panchayats) | T → shade/shelter (Urban Local Bodies)
Art. 338A
"338A — 89th Amendment"
NCST was executive body (1978) → made constitutional by 89th Amendment. The "A" in 338A is for this additional (constitutional) body.
16(4A) vs 16(4B)
"A = Aarakshan in promotion | B = Backlog"
Art. 16(4A) → Promotion mein reservation | Art. 16(4B) → Backlog posts carry forward

✅ Art. 342(1) — President consults the Governor and notifies STs via public notification. Art. 366(25) only defines the term.

✅ Originally 46, but 3 were removed in 2003 → now 43 tribes in MP. Odisha has the most: 62. Total in India: 705 (as per 1950 order).

A
Article 338A — NCST
B
Article 334 — Duration of reservation
C

✅ The 89th Constitutional Amendment created the separate National Commission for Scheduled Tribes (NCST) under Art. 338A. Before this, it was an executive body since 1978.

✅ Art. 164(1) mandates a dedicated Tribal Welfare Minister in Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, and Odisha — states with large ST populations.

✅ 104th Amendment (2019) → Extended SC/ST reservation to 2030 under Art. 334 AND abolished the Anglo-Indian reserved seats in Lok Sabha and state assemblies.
