What is ICT?
01
▾

IT — Information Technology

  • Deals with storage, retrieval, processing of data
  • Tools: Computers, Servers, Software
  • Focus: Managing information

CT — Communication Technology

  • Deals with transmission of information
  • Tools: Radio, Telephone, Internet
  • Focus: Transferring information
💡
ICT = IT + CT. When information technology and communication technology are combined, the result is ICT. The internet is the perfect example of ICT in action.

🔴 Transducer (ट्रांसड्यूसर)

  • Converts one form of energy to another
  • E.g., Microphone (sound → electrical), Speaker (electrical → sound)

🔵 Amplifier (एम्प्लीफायर)

  • Increases the power/strength of a wave signal
  • Does NOT change form — just makes signal stronger
⚡
Wave Frequency Order (Low → High): Radio Waves → Infrared → Visible Light → UV → X-Rays → Gamma Rays. Wavelength is opposite (highest for Radio, lowest for Gamma).

Exam Focus: VPN provides encryption and hides IP address. It creates a secure "tunnel" over the public internet. Most discussed network type in recent exams.

Network Topologies

BUS
🚌 Bus Topology
All nodes connected to a single central cable. If main cable fails, entire network fails.
STAR
⭐ Star Topology
All nodes connected to a central Hub/Switch. Most common. Hub fails → all fail.
RING
💍 Ring Topology
Each node connected to two neighbors forming a ring. Data travels in one direction.
TREE
🌳 Tree Topology
Combination of Bus + Star. Hierarchical structure. Used in social networks.
MESH
🕸️ Mesh Topology
Every node connected to every other node. Most reliable, but expensive.
HYBRID
🔀 Hybrid Topology
Combination of two or more topologies. Flexible and scalable.
📌
Social Networks use Tree Topology (or Graph) — this was directly asked in MPPSC exam.

Switching Techniques
05
▾

📞 Circuit Switching

A dedicated physical path is established between sender and receiver before communication begins.

  • Physical connection must exist
  • Resources reserved for entire call
  • Inefficient if no data flowing
📱 Telephone / PSTN

📦 Packet Switching

Message is broken into small packets. Each packet travels independently and reassembles at destination.

  • Each packet has source + destination address
  • Packets may take different routes
  • Reassembled at receiver end
  • Also called Store & Forward
🌐 Internet / TCP

✉️ Message Switching

Entire message is stored at intermediate nodes, then forwarded when path is free. No dedicated path.

  • Message stored, then forwarded
  • Delay possible
  • Not important for exam focus
📧 Email
💡
Key exam facts: (1) TCP/IP uses Packet Switching. (2) Packet switching = "Store and Forward" technique. (3) Each data packet has its own source address and destination address.

Robotics & Laws of Robotics
08
▾
🎯
PYQ (MPPSC 2021-22): "Which organization developed the Indian robot named Vyom Mitra?"
Answer: ISRO (for the Gaganyaan human spaceflight mission)

Asimov's 3 Laws of Robotics

1
First Law — Safety
A robot may not injure a human being, or allow a human to come to harm through inaction
2
Second Law — Obedience
A robot must obey orders given by humans, except where it conflicts with the First Law
3
Third Law — Self-preservation
A robot must protect its own existence, as long as this doesn't conflict with Laws 1 or 2

Vyom Mitra — Key Facts

  • Humanoid robot by ISRO
  • For Gaganyaan mission (India's first human spaceflight)
  • Purpose: Test life support systems before sending humans
  • Can speak, recognize faces, operate switches

Famous Robots (GK)

  • Sophia — AI robot by Hanson Robotics (Saudi Arabia citizenship)
  • ASIMO — Honda's humanoid robot
  • Atlas — Boston Dynamics robot
  • Manav — India's first 3D printed humanoid
